History

Once upon a time, nestled in the heart of a lush valley surrounded by towering hills that resembled scoops of ice cream topped with sprinkles of wildflowers, stood the Candy Hills Hotel. This enchanting establishment wasn't always the haven of sweetness and warmth it is known today. Its history is as rich and colorful as the candy-themed decorations that adorn its walls. The story of Candy Hills Hotel began in the early 1920s with a visionary confectioner, Charles Sweetwater, who dreamed of creating a retreat where the joy of candy could be experienced beyond the taste buds. Charles, a renowned candy maker known for his inventive flavors and delightful sweets, envisioned a place where families could escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life and immerse themselves in a world of whimsy and wonder. In 1925, with the support of his devoted family and the craftsmanship of the local community, Charles transformed a sprawling Victorian mansion, once the estate of a local magnate, into the first iteration of the Candy Hills Hotel. The mansion's intricate architecture and vast gardens provided the perfect canvas, blending elegance with the playful essence of a candy land.

The hotel quickly became a sensation, attracting guests from far and wide. Its reputation was built on exceptional service, fantastical decor, and an ambiance that whisked guests away to a dreamlike state. Each room was themed after different confections, from the Chocolate Suite with its rich, cocoa-infused furnishings to the Gummy Bear Garden where children could play among giant gummy sculptures. In the 1940s and 1950s, the Candy Hills Hotel saw its golden years. It hosted lavish parties, enchanting tea gatherings, and was the preferred getaway for celebrities seeking privacy and indulgence. The hotel expanded, adding new wings and attractions, including a candy-themed amusement park and a confectionery museum showcasing the history of sweets from around the world. Today, the Candy Hills Hotel stands as a testament to the vision of its founder, Charles Sweetwater, and the enduring appeal of a world crafted from joy, imagination, and a pinch of sugar. It remains a beloved destination for families, couples, and anyone looking to indulge in the sweet side of life.
